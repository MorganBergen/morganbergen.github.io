I invented a simple recipe for teamwork success called “The 3 C’s” - Cooperation, Coordination, and Communication. If any of these three elements are missing or are out of balance within the team, a successful goal execution will be diminished, if not, unattained.  

Introduction to Technology & Innovation was a seminar course centered on engineering and inter-disciplinary entrepreneurship. The final consisted of competing in the KOCH Innovation Challenge, where our team competed to invent products and technologies for technically feasible and potentially profitable prototypes, with the winner earning early round funding or scholarships. 

My team prototyped a high-level RFID detection mobile application that would allow you to receive push notifications when RFID skimming or a potential data theft was detected. Our team was running low on the element of coordination due to the fact that our version control for files kept changing naming conventions, which resulted in turning in the incorrect document for our final. 

[koch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Yre8zgZ0etA)

It was not until one of us went back to the submission board to find that we needed to have this file replaced and locate the correct “final version”. Luckily we had submitted the final project a week before the deadline, so if it wasn’t for prioritizing a culture of steadfastness and urgency to finish early, we wouldn’t have caught the error and resubmitted on time. Although this was a trivial mistake, learning how to create a culture of priorities was critical to our cooperation whenever our coordination was falling short.
